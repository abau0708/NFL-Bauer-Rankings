Week 2 Analysis

Week 2 brought some major shakeups and clarified who the real contenders are. The Colts made history by not punting through two games, while the Chiefs' struggles became impossible to ignore. Several teams showed their true colors in crucial road tests.

Biggest Riser

Indianapolis Colts (+14) - I think this Colts team is kind of legit. The offensive line is fantastic, Jonathan Taylor looks like a top RB, and Tyler Warren looks like a top TE. As long as Daniel Jones stays within himself and doesn't turn the ball over, this team will compete for the AFC South.

Honorary Mention: LA Rams (+7) - The Rams are a team nobody is going to want to play come January. Elite head coach, hall of fame QB, elite defense, and 2 stud WRs. If they can stay healthy then it's super bowl or bust this season.

Biggest Faller

Cincinnati Bengals (-9) - Really tough break for the Bengals and for Joe Burrow. Any time your franchise QB goes out with an injury it sets everything back, but especially when he's out for 3 months. The schedule is grueling, and I don't see Jake Browning doing enough to keep the Bengals above water while Burrow heals from a turf toe injury.

Honorary Mention: Kansas City Chiefs (-9) - The run game is a major concern. I will go on record and say I think the Giants will upset the Chiefs on Sunday Night and when that happens, it will be time to go into full panic mode.

Next Week Watch

Rams @ Eagles - A rematch of the divisional round last year, and it feels like these two teams are destined to meet again the playoffs. This should be an outstanding game that I think the Rams will squeak out.

Division Battle

Broncos @ Chargers - Feels like a game that will have major ramifications for the AFC West. If the Chargers win, they move to 3-0 while the Broncos will move to 1-2. On the other hand, with a Broncos victory both teams go to 2-1, opening up the division for a season long battle. Sean Peyton v Jim Harbaugh. Saddle up. Give me the Chargers in a low scoring game.
